Culture of Kubang Kerian:



1-Kampung Pulau Tengah, Kubang Kerian, Kelantan

Following the MCO, Kampung Pulau Tengah`, once a tranquil small community, has become a favourite with local tourists yearning for natural scenery and an opportunity to explore local customs. You can take a 3km river trail by boat from Kampung Pulau Tengah to Kampung Pulau Melaka, stopping at three villages to observe local community activities ranging from harvesting nipah (palm fruits) to searching for lokan (freshwater mussels) and admiring the natural beauty of Sungai Pengkalan Datu. Then, at night, you may go to the firefly park and take a moonlit boat ride while being surrounded by nature's light show. Finally, the Astacona Riverview Island Restaurant, which offers the unique sensation of dining on a tree, is open for lunch and dinner.



2-Min House Camp, Kubang Kerian, Kelantan

Min House Camp, located on the outskirts of Kota Bharu town, offers a lovely nature experience, particularly for those who appreciate exploring natural sites and discovering flora and wildlife. Min House Camp, set among lush tropical foliage, offers a variety of activities with a focus on nature and local culture. One of its biggest attractions is firefly watching, which allows you to witness nature's light show in its natural setting. Other activities you can do include Making handicrafts, cooking classes, playing traditional games, and attending cultural performances like wayang kulit (shadow play) and dikir barat (a Kelantanese traditional dance).





Heritage of Kubang Kerian:


1-Istana Negeri (GPS: 6.08206, 102.2778)

Istana Negeri (GPS: 6.08206, 102.2778) is the Sultan of Kelantan's royal residence in the Kubang Kerian area of Kota Bharu, Kelantan. The palace's construction began in 1974 and was finished in 1978.




2-Makam Tok Kenali (GPS: 6.08816, 102.27774)

Makam Tok Kenali (GPS coordinates: 6.08815, 102.27774) is the grave of a religious teacher in the Kota Bharu suburb of Kubang Kerian, Kelantan. The tomb is located in Mukim Kenali's Muslim cemetery. Tok Kenali is the nickname given to Muhammad Yusuf bin Ahmad (1868-1933), an ulama (Muslim religious teacher and scholar) who has expanded Muslim thought throughout the world. He is regarded as one of Kelantan's most powerful ulamas. Tok Kenali was born in the Kubang Kerian settlement of Kampung Kenali. His father worked on the farm. He studied the Quranic scriptures with his grandfather, Tok Leh. The community where Tok Kenali was laid to rest is today known as Kampung Kubur Tok Kenali.




3-Masjid Al Sultan Ismail Petra (GPS: 6.09405, 102.27403)

Masjid Al-Sultan Ismail Petra is a mosque in Kubang Kerian, Kelantan (GPS coordinates: 6.09405, 102.27403). The mosque can be found on Jalan Kota Bharu-Kuala Terengganu, also known as Federal Route 3. This is one of Kelantan's largest mosques. A big prayer hall with a bowl-shaped dome may be found here. The prayer hall is surrounded by four minarets.




4-Masjid At-Taqwa (GPS: 6.05431, 102.28103)

Masjid At-Taqwa (GPS: 6.05431, 102.28103) is a mosque in the Islamic town of Pasir Tumboh in Kubang Kerian, Kelantan. The mosque is located on Federal Route 3.
